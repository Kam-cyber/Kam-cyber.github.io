Travel to BRAZIL


Brazil is a vast country in South America that stretches from the Amazon Basin in the north to the vineyards and massive Iguazu Falls in the south.


Brazil has 203,100,000 inhabitants and is the most populous country in Latin America. It was conquered by Portugal and, after its independence, it retained Portuguese as the official language, except for some indigenous populations who still preserve their original language and live in remote places in the Amazon.


Brazil's flora and fauna has greater biodiversity than any other country on the planet, especially in the Amazon rainforest. It is the largest jungle in the world, and has an area of almost 7 million km2.

Culture of Brazil


The culture of Brazil is characterized by the influence of indigenous, African, European cultures (such as Italian) and other countries in the world such as Japan (Brazil is home to the largest Japanese colony outside of Japan). One of the main reflections of Brazilian cultural diversity is music: African folklore in Portuguese, samba, axé and bossa nova are some representative genres. His main references are Tom Jobim, Caetano Veloso, Vinicius de Moraes, Chico Burque, Gilberto Gil, Elis Regina and Gal Costa.


Carnival and capoeira are a central part of the Brazilian musical tradition. The carnival acquired worldwide recognition due to the impressive display of the celebration and the quality of the spectacle of the different troupes that parade to the beat of traditional music. Capoeira, for its part, emerged as a martial art technique and, over time, became an expression of Brazilian national culture.

You can eat food like:

  • Moqueca


      • Moqueca' or muqueca' is a fish stew made with onion, chili, tomato, coriander leaves and malagueta, all made with palm oil and coconut milk. It is made slowly, and no water is added.

    • Curao


      • Curau is a sweet Brazilian custard-like dessert made from pressed green corn juice, cooked with milk and sugar.

    • Galinhada


      • Galinhada is a typical dish from Goiás ( Brazil ). It consists of cooked rice and cooked chicken in pieces. It is seasoned with saffron (which gives the rice its typical yellow color), vinaigrette ( optional and on the side) and feijão tutu . A typical galinhada contains catolé palm ( guariroba , a type of bitter palm heart) and pequi.
